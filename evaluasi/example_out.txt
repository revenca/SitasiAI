Azimuth-scanning MIMO radar can steer its transmit beam without per-element phase shifters by

ABSTRAK HIPOTETIS (HyDE, temp 0,7):
This paper presents a novel approach to azimuth-scanning MIMO radar systems that eliminates the
need for per-element phase shifters by employing a signal-circulation methodology. We propose a
coded signal transmission strategy that leverages time-delay differences among antenna elements to
achieve equivalent phase steering. Our experimental setup utilizes a 16-element linear array with
a frequency range of 2-4 GHz, implementing a custom-coded waveform designed for optimal Doppler
tolerance and spatial resolution. Extensive simulations and field tests demonstrate that our
method achieves a peak steering accuracy improvement of 25% over traditional phase-shifting
techniques, while maintaining robust target detection capabilities across varying environmental
conditions. These findings underscore the potential of coded signal circulation in enhancing the
performance and scalability of MIMO radar systems.
