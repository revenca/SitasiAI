--- ABSTRAK HIPOTESIS (HyDE, temp 0.7) ---
This paper presents a novel recommendation system designed to enhance internal business
processes by leveraging the COBIT 2019 framework. We employ a hybrid methodology that
integrates graph algorithms for mapping inter-entity relationships and semantic
similarity measurements to align enterprise goals with management objectives.
Furthermore, we utilize Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks combined with Term
Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) techniques to analyze data and recommend
targeted activities. The system was evaluated in a real-world business environment,
demonstrating a significant optimization of IT management practices, increased
operational efficiency, and improved regulatory compliance. Additionally, we introduce a
user-friendly graphical interface powered by Neo4j, which allows stakeholders to
visualize intricate relationships within the COBIT framework, thus facilitating informed
decision-making. Our findings contribute to advancing IT governance practices, providing
organizations with an automated tool to refine IT strategies and effectively achieve
business objectives.

management by leveraging Neo4j to model COBIT 2019 elements. We propose a dynamic graph-
